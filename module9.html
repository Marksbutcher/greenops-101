Section 1

The regulatory landscape has shifted — and it will keep shifting.

Sustainability is no longer voluntary for enterprise technology organisations. A series of interconnected frameworks now reshape what IT must disclose, measure, and demonstrate.

Paris Agreement & SBTi

IT estates are inside the target boundary

Corporate sustainability commitments are increasingly anchored to the Paris Agreement through science-based targets. For most large enterprises, the technology estate is material enough to sit inside that SBTi boundary. Technology leaders are directly on the hook for delivery — not adjacent to it.

EU Taxonomy

Determines access to sustainable capital

For EU-based or EU-funded organisations, Taxonomy alignment affects green financing access and lending terms. Data centre and ICT investments must meet specific technical screening criteria. Non-aligned investments face increasing cost-of-capital disadvantage.

CSRD — Double Materiality

IT data must be assurable

CSRD requires both financial risk and environmental impact to be reported. Digital footprint data must be capable of audit. Scope 3 must be included where material. For technology estates, it almost always is. Spend-based proxies are insufficient at assurance grade. Silence is not a safe position.

TCFD

Physical and transition climate risk

Physical risk: data centres in exposed locations face operational disruption from extreme weather, heat, and water stress. Transition risk: stranded assets, regulatory shifts, changing capital allocation. Both require technology leaders to understand their infrastructure's climate risk profile.

Section 2

CSRD double materiality — what it means for IT.

Double materiality is the requirement to report from both directions: how sustainability issues affect the organisation financially, and what impact the organisation has on the environment and society.

The IT data quality implication

CSRD requires data that is assurable. Spend-based Scope 3 proxies, portfolio-average PUE figures, and self-declared supplier claims will not meet the evidentiary standard required for assurance-grade disclosure. The data quality investment is not optional — it is the compliance requirement.

Sustainable finance as a practical lever.

Knowledge Check · Module 9 · Q1

An organisation's sustainability report states that its data centre operations are powered by 100% renewable electricity, citing Renewable Energy Certificates. Under CSRD and the GHG Protocol, what is the most precise statement about what this claim represents?

Select an answer to reveal the explanation.

✓ Correct: Option B

Under the GHG Protocol, organisations report Scope 2 using both the location-based method (reflecting actual grid carbon intensity) and the market-based method (reflecting contractual instruments like RECs). The REC claim is valid for market-based reporting — but it does not change the physical grid that powers the facility.

CSRD requires both methods to be disclosed. An organisation reporting only the market-based figure, without also disclosing the location-based intensity, is providing an incomplete picture. The location-based figure is the physically honest number — the one GreenOps operational decisions should be based on.

Knowledge Check · Module 9 · Q2

A technology director says: "CSRD is a European regulation so it doesn't apply to us — we're headquartered outside the EU." Under what circumstances might CSRD still be relevant to this organisation?

Select an answer to reveal the explanation.

✓ Correct: Option B

CSRD directly applies to EU-headquartered organisations and to non-EU companies with significant EU revenue above specified thresholds. But the indirect reach extends further: any organisation that is a material supplier to a CSRD-obligated entity will face sustainability data requests — because those entities are required to cover material Scope 3 emissions, including supply chain.

This supply chain pull effect means the regulatory boundary is effectively wider than the direct applicability of the regulation. Organisations that sell to EU-based enterprises, access EU capital markets, or have significant EU operations should treat CSRD as material to their governance planning.
