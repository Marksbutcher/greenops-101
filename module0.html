What this course is

Operational discipline. Not ideology.

If you are here, you can probably sense that digital sustainability is becoming genuinely important for technology organisations — but most of what you have come across is either too vague, too preachy, or completely detached from how IT actually works.

The GreenOps definition

"GreenOps is the discipline of making digital consumption visible, governable, and optimisable."

Not a side project. Not an annual report. Not an offsetting strategy. Operational excellence applied to a constraint that is becoming more real every year.

This course is
  • ✓A practical foundation in operational reality and consumption discipline
  • ✓Grounded in how IT actually works — engineering, governance, cost, risk
  • ✓Built for practitioners and leaders across every technology function
  • ✓Technical enough to be credible, strategic enough to be actionable
This course is not
  • ✗A climate science lecture or a sustainability marketing script
  • ✗A session on how to feel better about technology
  • ✗Another annual report exercise or a set of aspirational targets
  • ✗A replacement for specialist depth in any single domain
Technology is not the villain. Unmanaged consumption is.

Digital infrastructure is essential to modern life. The problem is unmanaged consumption at scale — lazy defaults, waste that has been normalised because the cost and the physical impact are hidden behind layers of abstraction. When something feels consequence-free, people stop asking whether it needs to happen at all. GreenOps changes that.

Who this course is for

If you make decisions about technology, this is for you.

The course is organised into three tracks, each calibrated for a different primary audience. You do not have to take every module in sequence — each is coherent on its own. That said, if you are new to this subject, starting from the beginning gives the strongest foundation.

How to get the most from it

Three things that make the difference.

The course is designed to be taken actively, not passively. The Pause and Reflect prompts are not filler. The knowledge checks are self-checks, not exams. The value comes from connecting the content to your own estate, your own decisions, your own constraints.

Pause and Reflect prompts

When you see a Pause and Reflect: stop, take two or three minutes, and write your answers down. Connect the content to your own organisation — your estate, your decisions, your constraints. If you do this consistently, you will finish with concrete, specific observations rather than just a new vocabulary.

Knowledge checks

The knowledge checks at the end of each module are self-checks, not exams. If you cannot answer a question comfortably, that is a signal to revisit that section or flag it for when you have encountered more context. They are designed to reveal gaps, not to test recall under pressure.

Navigation

You do not have to take every module in sequence — each is coherent on its own. The track structure makes it easy to focus on what is most relevant to your role. The most valuable next step after any module is to go back to the one that challenged you most and answer the questions you skipped.
