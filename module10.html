The Business Case

Value, Risk, and Strategic Opportunity

By the end of this module you will:

  • 01Articulate the three dimensions of value in a sustainable IT business case: financial, risk, and non-financial
  • 02Identify which financial levers — cost reduction, capex efficiency, green financing, revenue — are material to your organisation
  • 03Map regulatory, reputational, supply chain, and climate risks that the business case must address
  • 04Frame the non-financial value case: talent, engagement, investor expectations, customer requirements, and operational productivity
  • 05Build a business case that wins in a room — with evidence, not assumptions

Section 1

Three Dimensions of Value

A credible business case is built on three pillars: financial value, risk management, and non-financial value. All three matter. All three need to be present.

Tap each dimension to explore

Immediate Cost Reduction
Waste elimination — idle compute, over-provisioned storage, unnecessary licences — creates direct cost savings that are often large enough to fund the programme itself.
Improved Total Cost of Ownership
Evidence-based device refresh cycles, circular procurement prioritising repairability, and data minimisation all reduce long-term hardware and infrastructure costs.
Green Financing Access
Green bonds, sustainability-linked loans, and ESG-aligned investment are growing rapidly. Credible programmes unlock financing not available to those without them.
Revenue and Market Access
Enterprise procurement increasingly includes sustainability criteria. Public sector contracts mandate sustainable procurement. Evidence wins contracts that others lose.
Regulatory Compliance
CSRD, EU Taxonomy, and equivalent frameworks are expanding. Organisations without credible programmes face compliance gaps, regulatory penalties, and reputational damage.
Reputational Risk
Unsubstantiated sustainability claims are increasingly subject to legal challenge. Greenwashing creates exposure. Credible, auditable programmes build trust with stakeholders.
Supply Chain Risk
Concentrated, complex supply chains face geopolitical, material scarcity, and regulatory disruption. Organisations that map dependencies manage this risk more effectively.
Physical Climate Risk
Data centre and network infrastructure in flood zones, heat-stressed regions, or water-scarce areas face operational risk. TCFD-aligned assessment is essential.
Talent Attraction and Retention
Sustainability performance is a real factor in technology professional decisions, especially for those earlier in careers. Credible programmes attract and retain talent more effectively.
Employee Engagement
People who feel their organisation is taking meaningful action are more engaged. The difference between credible action and performative communication matters internally as much as externally.
Investor and Stakeholder Expectations
ESG factors are integrated into investment analysis. Institutional investors apply sustainability screens. Better ESG performance influences cost of capital and investor access.
Operational Productivity
Lower digital waste means cleaner, simpler, more manageable estates. Engineers spend less time managing legacy complexity and more time building capability that matters.

Which dimension will win in your room?

Different audiences are motivated by different dimensions. Finance cares about cost reduction and capital efficiency. Risk and compliance teams care about regulatory exposure. HR and talent teams care about recruitment and engagement. The strongest business cases address all three — but lead with the dimension that your audience will listen to.

Which risks matter most to your business?

Risk profile varies by organisation. A capital-intensive player with strong ESG requirements cares deeply about stranded assets and green financing. A B2B technology vendor cares acutely about customer procurement pressure. Start with the risks that your board cares about, and build the business case around those.

Which of the following best represents the three pillars of a credible sustainable IT business case?

✓ Correct — Option B

A credible business case is built on three complementary dimensions. Financial value addresses the immediate and long-term cost case. Risk management addresses regulatory, reputational, supply chain, and climate risks. Non-financial value addresses talent, engagement, investor expectations, and operational productivity. All three need to be present — but different audiences are motivated by different dimensions.

Knowledge Check · Module 10 · Q2

Which of the following is NOT typically a component of the financial value case for sustainable IT?

Select an answer to reveal the explanation.

✓ Correct — Option C

Reduced employee attrition is a non-financial value driver. It's real, it matters, and it should be in the business case. But it's not typically modelled as direct financial value in the same way that cost reduction, green financing access, and revenue/market access are. Employee engagement and talent retention belong in the non-financial value pillar.

Knowledge Check · Module 10 · Q3

An organisation discovers that 12% of its cloud infrastructure is idle and could be shut down for £1.8m annual savings. What dimension of the business case does this most directly address?

Select an answer to reveal the explanation.

✓ Correct — Option A

While idle resource cleanup has secondary benefits (reduced complexity, better engineer focus), it primarily demonstrates financial value through immediate, measurable cost reduction. This is the strongest argument for starting with waste in the business case — it's easy to measure, it's easy to defend, and it funds the longer-term programme through immediate recovery.

Module 10 — Key Takeaways

◆
Three dimensions, one case.

Financial value, risk management, and non-financial value are all necessary. Audiences vary — lead with the dimension your board will listen to, but include all three.

◆
Start with waste, win with savings.

Cost reduction from waste elimination is the fastest credibility-builder. Most programmes recover their full cost in 9–12 months.

◆
Risk is a lever, not an obligation.

Regulatory, reputational, supply chain, and climate risks are real. Organisations that haven't addressed them are exposed. Frame sustainability as risk management, not just environmental virtue.

◆
Talent and engagement are financial drivers.

Sustainability performance affects recruitment, retention, and employee engagement. In a competitive talent market, this is material.

◆
Evidence, not assumptions.

Build the business case with data — waste audits, risk assessments, competitive analysis. The strongest case is the one you can defend in a room.
