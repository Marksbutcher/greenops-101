The GreenOps definition

"GreenOps is the discipline of making digital consumption visible, governable, and optimisable."

Visible

You cannot govern what you cannot see. Measurement, baselines, and attribution are the starting point — not the output.

Governable

Ownership, accountability, and decision forums. Named owners for named services. Sustainability embedded in the same governance loops as cost and performance.

Optimisable

Efficiency, then elimination. Unit metrics improving quarter on quarter. Avoidance becoming the default, not an aspiration.

The discipline, stated plainly

Digital is physical. Every service consumes energy. Many consume water. All consume materials somewhere in the chain. The question is not whether digital grows — it will. The question is whether it grows intelligently. Technology is not the villain. Unmanaged consumption is.

Section 3

The operating context is changing.

The era of treating digital infrastructure as consequence-free is ending. Not because of regulation alone — though regulation is arriving. Because the physical constraints are becoming real. Power availability. Water availability. Supply chain pressure. The cost of AI at scale. These are not abstract future risks.

Five shifts that are already shaping technology strategy — click any to see what it means operationally

Digital sustainability is not a destination — it is an operating discipline. The organisations that treat it as such will be better positioned on cost, risk, talent, and regulatory compliance than those that treat it as a compliance exercise or a communications strategy.

Organisations that build this discipline now are building an advantage

Not just in reporting terms — in how they run. They waste less. They have better data. They make better decisions. They are more resilient to supply chain and regulatory disruption. And they are better positioned for a market that is increasingly rewarding genuine operational performance over stated ambition.

Knowledge Check · Final · Q1

An organisation has reduced data centre PUE from 1.6 to 1.4 and is reporting this as a sustainability success. However, total data centre energy consumption has risen by 22% over the same period due to AI workload growth. Which statement best characterises the situation?

Correct. This is the rebound effect in practice — efficiency improvement at the unit level does not prevent absolute growth if demand is outpacing the gain. Reporting the PUE improvement without the total consumption trend is misleading by omission. Both dimensions must be present for the disclosure to be honest. This is exactly what regulators and auditors will scrutinise under CSRD double materiality.
Not quite. The core issue here is not whether PUE and consumption are related metrics — they are. The problem is selective disclosure. Reporting an efficiency improvement while total consumption rises materially, without stating the total trend, creates a false impression of overall progress. Intensity metrics and absolute totals must both be disclosed.

Knowledge Check · Final · Q2

A technology leader has been asked by the board to explain the company's digital sustainability programme. The programme has a strategy document, a quarterly sustainability committee, and an annual report. It has no named service owners, no per-service carbon intensity metrics, and no waste removal backlog. What is the most accurate assessment?

Correct. Strategy documents, governance committees, and annual reports are reporting infrastructure — they create a record. The three operational governance artefacts (top-ten service register with named owners, vendor evidence register, waste removal backlog) are what create accountability. Without them, sustainability remains a communications function. The test is whether a named individual can be held accountable for a specific carbon intensity figure in an operational forum — not whether a report has been published.
Not quite. The gap here is not scale, resourcing, or timing — it is the absence of operational accountability. A sustainability committee reviewing an annual report is reporting infrastructure. Named service owners accountable for carbon intensity KPIs in a quarterly operational forum is governance. The three artefacts that close this gap are the top-ten service register, the vendor evidence register, and the waste removal backlog. These can be started immediately with imperfect data.
