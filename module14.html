The core mental model

Digital sustainability is operational excellence under constraint.

Not ideology. Not a communications programme. Not a poster campaign. Digital is physical. Every service consumes energy — many consume water, all consume materials. GreenOps is the discipline that treats those facts as operational reality rather than sustainability narrative.

GreenOps is the discipline of making digital consumption visible, governable, and optimisable.
Level 1
Reporting
The rear-view mirror. Foundation, but not the goal.
Tells you what happened. Measurement, baselines, inventories, dashboards, disclosures. Essential for decision-making. Insufficient as a stopping point. Too many programmes stop here.
Level 2
Optimisation
Operational discipline. Requires governance to sustain.
Reduces waste in what you already run. Utilisation improvement, right-sizing, carbon-aware scheduling, efficient code patterns. Without demand management, efficiency gains are reabsorbed.
Level 3
Avoidance
Highest leverage. Stops unnecessary demand materialising.
Architecture decisions, portfolio rationalisation, data minimisation, not running heavyweight AI where lighter approaches work. The carbon you never generate beats any optimisation you could apply to it.

What to do in the next 30 days

Three artefacts. That is all you need to start.

Don't wait for perfect data. One honest register is worth more than ten unreviewed questionnaires. The artefact that does not yet exist is the one to start.

01
Top-ten service register
Identify the ten most material digital services in your estate. For each: assign a named owner, define one unit metric (carbon per transaction, per session, per GB), record the primary consumption driver, and note your confidence level in the current figure.
Start: list the ten services with the highest estimated footprint — even with low confidence. Assign an owner to each. Set a review date.
02
Vendor evidence register
For your key suppliers — cloud, hardware, managed services — record what evidence you have and what is missing. Note the assurance tier (self-declared, reviewed, or assured). Note your next evidence request.
Start: list your top 5 technology suppliers by spend. Note what you currently hold for each. Send your first formal evidence request to the one with the largest Scope 3 exposure.
03
Waste removal backlog
List the waste items you already know about or strongly suspect: idle instances, zombie resources, orphaned storage, applications with no active users, fixed refresh cycles. Assign a named owner and target date to each item.
Start: one waste category, one owner, one target. Put it in your operational tracking system — not a separate sustainability spreadsheet.
