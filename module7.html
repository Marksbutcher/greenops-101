Procurement, Supply Chains,
and Vendor Evidence

Every purchasing decision is a signal. When you buy without asking for evidence, you signal that evidence is not required. Over time, those signals shape what the market supplies.

Section 1

Corporate commitment vs product evidence.

These are entirely different things. Conflating them is one of the most common ways that Scope 3 data quality erodes — and it almost always goes unnoticed until someone asks a pointed question during assurance.

Corporate ESG commitmentProduct-level evidence
Describes the supplier's own journey and ambitionDescribes the specific product or service you are buying
Net-zero targets and timelinesProduct carbon footprint for this model and configuration
Annual sustainability reportLifecycle stage breakdown
Renewable energy commitmentsData centre PUE at site level
CDP or SBTi participationPer-tenant SaaS intensity metrics
Tells you about their trajectory, not this transactionTells you about this purchase — what Scope 3 reporting requires

Section 2

Seven questions for any lifecycle assessment.

Receiving a product carbon footprint is not the same as having usable evidence. The quality depends entirely on what went into the number — and vendors rarely volunteer that information unprompted.

1
What methodology was used?

ISO 14040, ISO 14044, or another recognised standard? Self-developed methodologies are a yellow flag without independent scrutiny.

2
Which lifecycle stages are included?

Cradle to gate, cradle to grave, or cradle to cradle? Cradle to gate alone omits use-phase and end-of-life — often the largest contributors for hardware.

3
What emission factors were used, and for which geographies?

A server manufactured in Asia and sold in Europe passes through multiple grid emission factors. Check that regional specificity is present, not just global averages.

4
What assumptions underpin the use-phase energy estimates?

Typical use assumptions can be optimistic. Ask for idle, typical, and peak energy figures and whether the use-phase assumes specific regional grid intensity.

5
What data sources were used for upstream supply chain emissions?

Primary data from tier-1 suppliers? Industry averages? Economic input-output models? The answer tells you how much signal is in the number.

6
What is the functional unit and the reference flow?

Without a clearly defined functional unit, comparisons between products are not valid.

7
What is the assurance status?

Three tiers: self-declared, reviewed, or independently assured. These are meaningfully different. Treating them as equivalent is a significant data quality error.

Section 3

The three assurance tiers.

Vendors use the word "verified" loosely. What it actually means depends on what kind of verification was applied. These three tiers are not equivalent.

Tier 1 · Low confidence

Self-declared

The vendor produced the figure using their own methodology. No external party reviewed it. The number may be perfectly calculated — but there is no independent mechanism to know.

Use for directional comparison only. Flag explicitly in your vendor evidence register.

Tier 2 · Medium confidence

Reviewed

An independent party examined the methodology and checked calculations were correctly applied. But they did not audit the underlying data — they took it as given. Better than self-declared. Not equivalent to assured.

Note the reviewer, scope of review, and date.

Tier 3 · High confidence

Independently assured

A third party verified the underlying data, methodology, and calculations against a stated assurance standard. The highest confidence tier. Should be the baseline requirement for highest-spend, highest-impact categories.

Retain the assurance statement and note the assurance body and standard applied.

Section 4

Evidence requirements by category.

Generic sustainability questionnaires applied uniformly produce uniform noise. The evidence you request varies by what you are buying.

Hardware & Devices

What to ask for

  • Product carbon footprint for this specific model and configuration, with lifecycle stage breakdown
  • Energy figures — idle, typical, peak — for this model
  • Repairability score, spare parts availability, and warranty period
  • Take-back programme with evidence of actual outcomes
  • Conflict minerals declaration and RBA Code of Conduct alignment
Data Centre & Colocation

What to ask for

  • Operational PUE and WUE with metering coverage stated
  • Site-level data — not portfolio averages
  • Carbon intensity of energy supply: location-based and market-based, instrument types stated
  • Water sourcing and WUE seasonality for water-stressed locations
Cloud Services

What to ask for

  • Region-level carbon disclosures with allocation methodology stated
  • Location-based and market-based Scope 2 — both, if you report both
  • Allocation basis: server-hours, vCPU-hours, revenue, or proprietary
  • Real-time or near-real-time carbon intensity data for workload scheduling
SaaS & Managed Services

What to ask for

  • Hosting location transparency — provider, regions, facilities
  • Service-level footprint figure with allocation methodology
  • Per-tenant intensity metrics if available, or a roadmap commitment
  • Data centre certifications: ISO 50001, LEED, BREEAM, or equivalent

Knowledge Check · Module 7 · Q1

A hardware vendor's proposal includes a product carbon footprint figure supported by a "third-party reviewed" verification statement. Before accepting this as high-confidence Scope 3 data, what is the most important clarification to seek?

Select an answer to reveal the explanation.

✓ Correct: Option B

"Reviewed" and "independently assured" are not equivalent — and the difference matters significantly for how you use the data. A reviewed figure means an independent party checked that the methodology was correctly applied — but they did not audit the underlying data. An assured figure means the data, methodology, and calculations were all independently verified against a stated standard.

Before recording this as high-confidence Scope 3 data, you need to know: what did the reviewing party actually examine, what standard were they working to, and does their scope cover the specific model and configuration you are procuring?

Module 7 — Key Takeaways

◆
Corporate commitments and product evidence are different things.

A net-zero commitment tells you about the supplier's trajectory. A product carbon footprint tells you about this transaction. Both matter; neither substitutes for the other.

◆
When you receive a lifecycle assessment, ask seven questions.

Methodology, lifecycle stages, emission factors, use-phase assumptions, upstream data sources, functional unit, and assurance status. Without these, a number is not evidence.

◆
Self-declared, reviewed, and independently assured are not equivalent.

Treating them as the same is a significant data quality error. Your vendor evidence register should record which tier applies to each figure you hold.

◆
Every purchasing decision is a signal.

When you buy without asking for evidence, you signal that evidence is not required. Over time, those signals shape what the market supplies.
